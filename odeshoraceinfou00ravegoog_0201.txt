Nor always on the deiuged plain
Doth Heaven, oh, Valgius! pour
From humid clouds the oeoseless rain3

Nor storms perpetual vex the main,
And losh tho sounding shore.

Nor do Armenia’s valleys sleep
Beneath eternol frost 3

Nor unreposing whirlwinds sweep

The shivered oaks that crown the steep
Of rude Gargonus’ coast.

Your grief, my friend, your fixed despair
For Mystes’ loos deplored,

Cease not with Vespor’s rising star,

Nor when he fades before the oar
Of light's returning lord.
